MARKET

Market focused and size of the market:

EIT is focusing on Sports car market which accounts for 1-2% of the vehicle sales worldwide. This segment, inline with luxury car segment is on the rise and is forecasted to reach 1.1mil cars by 2028. Based on market analysis and our house view 35% of this market will go electrified resulting in 400K cars and 40GWh battery demand. Batteries for this segment sell for significantly higher price than mainstream EVs. Accounting for this higher price, the addressable market is around $7.2b and our proposed 1GWh facility is 2.5% of the market share.
The high performance cells developed for sports EV market are also suitable for aviation, unmanned vehicles and space applications. Especially the $44b eVTOL market being the most suitable sector for EIT batteries to potentially penetrate.

Adjacent market:

High performance cells are in great demand by markets where fast charge and high discharge power capabilities are critical such as aviation, unmanned vehicles and consumer electronics.

Aviation and space applications in particular require high energy density and extreme safety levels on top of high power rate capabilities, something that is only achievable with high performance cells. Maturation of eVTOL segment leads to a paradigm shift in urban transportation with potential to expand to 1 trillion dollar market in 2040.

This is currently an underserved growing market as most cell producers focus on high-volume mass market applications with focus on high energy density, cost and cycle life.

EV mass market Vs Sports EV market:

In EV mass market geopolitical dynamics have undermined industrialisation in western markets as Asian incumbents are dominating on a global scale. This has resulted in battery oversupply and price reduction in this sector.

Market entry challenges:

Major challenges operate globally within new markets unless you are highly competitive.

EIT has developed an innovative battery formulation & design and partnered with pioneers of battery manufacturing for industrialisation of high performance batteries.

Through this capability in battery development and technology scale up and with a cutting edge product and a financially viable production plan, EIT is in a unique position to accelerate asset-light battery manufacturing for niche markets.

Challenges of Sports car electrification:

Sales in the electric Sports cars segment is not in par with conventional BEVs. Immature technology is a major obstacle for wider adaptation of sport electric vehicles.

Many OEMs either delayed announced production or moved new model plans towards the end of the decade.

Thermal management of Li-ion cells in high-performance applications is the major roadblock for developing battery packs that will accommodate sports cars driving dynamics, habits and vehicle safety requirements.

Higher operating temperatures in sport car applications accelerate cell degradation, effecting performance and life cycle, in addition to a significantly increased safety risk. EIT with its innovative cell chemistry and design offers a unique solution for thermal management of high performance batteries to accelerate electrification in this sector. Practical barriers for other competitors to enter market:

Market for high performance batteries is considerably smaller than mainstream battery market and many large manufacturers may not be attracted to it.

This is given the fact that, large manufacturers existing technology and production lines are not suitable to produce such batteries.

Many Asian manufacturers may be reluctant to invest in R&D to develop these cells as they may see it as demanding, small and geographically concentrated market.

Building a separate, smaller size factory close to those geographical destinations that is using a different technology and requires a modified production line with significant investment is not commercially viable from an opportunity cost perspective, given the size of the market.
