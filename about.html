ABOUT US

EIT is a Masdar based company in Abu Dhabi focusing on transitional technologies and industrialization.

By recognizing transitional bottlenecks, EIT is focusing on rapid industrialization and commercialization of advanced manufacturing in order to help facilitate the transition to Net Zero. Formed by industry experts and veterans with a strong track record, EIT’s vision is to build a green energy manufacturing ecosystem in the Middle East.

Mission

EIT makes the best use of strong geopolitical dynamics to put UAE and Abu Dhabi on the map as the leading nation focusing on transitional investments paving the way for a true green sustainable economy. Our mission is to accelerate greenfication by creating sticky IP for the next generation. EIT delivers best in class high performance batteries specifically tailored for luxury sports car and aviation segments.

How we do it:

Battery manufacturing scale up can turn into a costly and complex business.

Meanwhile mainstream EV market is becoming overly price competitive making battery production viable only if it’s produced at very large scales (10s GWh).

EIT has overcome this challenge by focusing on niche markets with less price sensitivity. Simultaneously by owning the manufacturing landscape and employing innovative production solutions to bring capital and operational expenditure down.

With higher sales prices and lower equipment & production cost, suddenly, small Gigafactories “asset light battery manufacturing” becomes financially viable.

EIT R&D centre:

The 600 sqm facilities is housing EIT pilot production line, testing and research capabilities. The centre has three main objectives:

  • Material screening and design/formulation development
  • Prototyping for testing/validation and process development & ramp up
  • Research and development on next generation of batteries and IP creation

EIT R&D centre plays a critical role in commercialization and technology transfer of high performance batteries in UAE.

Why UAE:

UAE has positioned itself in the forefront of energy transition by offering significant incentives for energy storage development and technology transfer. Additionally, UAE has one of the lowest tax levels for manufacturing sector and offers abundance of renewable energy that can be used during production process. Those benefits have been demonstrated before such as in production of Lykan hypercar in UAE and the current assembly line of the Rabdan electric vehicle.

Furthermore, UAE has very ambitious plans in the field of space, green aviation and unmanned autonomous vehicles creating a potentially significant additional demand from sectors such as eVTOL that EIT batteries can support. Other additional diversification opportunities such as cathode/anode production can also be explored benefiting from inexpensive energy sources. Overall, there is enormous appetite and attraction for the first gigafactory of the region.

Masdar in Abu Dhabi is the world's first zero-emission city. It attracts investment opportunities in international large-scale green projects and is actively exploring transformative acquisitions as part of its strategic plan to expand its footprint in the United States and Europe.

In-Country Value:

In line with national ICV program of UAE, EIT’s socio-economical roadmap aims to develop a new local industry by attracting foreign investments, diversifying the economy, creating valuable job opportunities in the private sector, contributing to the growth of the national GDP, enhancing spending on research and development (R&D) & advanced technologies and achieving strategic localisation of supply chain.
A summary of ICVs created by EIT is listed below:

  • Placing UAE on the map by building the first Gigafactory of the Middle East
  • Creating UAE homegrown industry with domestic IP for one of the most advanced technologies: next generation batteries for Electric vehicles
  • Creating 100 direct and 200 indirect high skill jobs
  • Drawing talent from rest of the world to UAE
  • Attracting foreign investment into the economy and contributing to UAE export
  • Contributing to the Emiratization of the economy
  • Upskilling UAE workforce from academia and oil/gas industry to green energy sector
  • Imbedding battery technology know-how into UAE research ecosystem
  • Accelerating UAE commitment of transition to renewable energies and decarbonization of the  economy
  • Creating value chain in the whole battery manufacturing cycle
  • Diverting fossil fuel usage to battery production ecosystem

Emiratization:

Starting with 20% Emirati workforce, EIT’s long term ambition is to operate with more than 50% of local workforce. This is realized through a robust knowledge transfer and upskilling program.

Grassroot research:

EIT PhD programs with Imperial College, MBZUAI University and Caltech on digital twin for battery manufacturing process and performance simulation is fundamental in establishing a battery research ecosystem for embedding of battery manufacturing know-how which in effect promotes homegrown industry that is backed by local IP and is created domestically.
