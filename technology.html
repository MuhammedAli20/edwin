TECHNOLOGY

Performance requirements of Sports EVs:

Batteries for Sports EVs need to be light: high energy density, deliver high power: high discharge rate, have fast charge capability: high charge rate and be safe.

Current battery packs are too heavy for Sports cars, get too hot and unsafe at high (acceleration) power and lack fast charge capability on track. EIT batteries on the other hand have overcome these challenges to give sports cars better speed, control, and performance compared to ICE vehicles.

Technology trend in sport EV market:

Porsche Mission X has set the trend for sports EV market. An all electric hypercar with immersion cooled pack concept, seeking 21700 cell and pack suppliers.

This proves where the market is heading and exposes the intrinsic demand that needs to be supplied.

About EIT Technology:

EIT employs some of the most sophisticated technologies to produce high performance batteries for sports car and adjacent markets. Such batteries need to have high energy density, deliver high charge and discharge power and be safe. EIT has already demonstrated its capability in producing high energy density cells that are in par with best energy cells in the market by combining high nickel NMC cathodes with high silicon graphite anodes. To achieve the power density required for fast charging acceleration and speed EIT is using some of the most innovative technologies available, namely dry electrode and tabless technology specifically designed for immersion cooled battery packs. These are the most effective technologies to ensure the highest power capabilities can be delivered using effective thermal management strategies.
Together with the most tailored formulation and mechanical design, EIT cells make a step change in performance of today batteries and improve battery manufacturing process by reducing production steps, energy usage and carbon footprint.

Cell format choice:

High energy pouch/prismatic format cells are mostly adapted in mainstream EV market to address range anxiety and cost reduction.

Sports EV batteries are increasingly leaning towards cylindrical format cells that offers superior cooling characteristics and technology maturity enabling two fundamental requirements of Sport EVs: performance and safety.

Immersion cooling technology:

Immersion (submerged, liquid) cooling of cells with dielectric liquid is the most reliable method for thermal management of high-performance cells and allow fast charging of as low as 8 mins with no cell cooling time required.

Other cooling technologies such as plate cooling would not meet the extremely high demanding performance requirements.

Immersion cooling technology has been widely used in data centres and super-computing industries.

Early adopters of liquid cooling technology in the world of Sport cars include: AMG, Porsch, Rimac, Koenigsegg, Faraday Future and McLaren.

Dry electrode technology:

In conventional wet method, solvents are mixed with active powder and binders to make slurry that is coated on current collecting foils and dried.

This method requires very long dryers (to keep up with production speeds) and a vigorous solvent recovery system.

Dry electrode technology eliminates the need for using solvents and therefore using dryer and solvent recovery system by mixing powders in dry form and directly applying to current collecting foils.

This not only removes two most energy and footprint consuming steps out of the process but also ensures uniform and effective blend of active material with the binder (improved conductivity), leading to 30% reduction in manufacturing cost, better power rate (due to better conductivity) and reduced CO2 footprint.

The technology was Edwin Institute of Technology, First Floor, Incubator Building, Masdar City, Abu Dhabi, UAE invented by Maxwell and has been scaled up and adopted by Tesla in their new models. Tesla is the only company to date that employs this technology for EV battery production.

The technology has several processing variations namely extrusion (employed by Tesla) and spray coating.

Tabless technology:

In conventional tab design the rolled electrodes (jelly roll) are connected with external terminals (tabs) to the poles.

In tabless design the need for external tabs is eliminated and jelly roll is directly connected to the poles.

This technology is adopted by Tesla for large format cells. EIT will be the first mover with intellectual property in this technology for 21700 format cells for EV applications.

Battery choice for aviation industry:

Battery performance needs 10X improvement (~3000Wh/kg) to be competitive with fueled commercial aircrafts making urban and short haul aviation an attractive market for electrification.

eVTOL is an extremely fast-growing market with around 100 companies already active in this sector, particularly attractive because there is no competition from fueled arial vehicles and current/near future technology (300-400 Wh/kg) is adequate.

Separately eVTOL batteries are natural candidates for space and UAV applications with extremely high price margins.

High-performance cells with pre-lithiation and high-silicon anodes are key enablers before introduction of a true solid-state (Li metal anode – 700 Wh/kg) solution, which is at least a decade away for commercialization with cycle life and power rate capability issues to solve - more advanced chemistries such as Li-S and Li-air are even in earlier infancy.

Technological barriers for other competitors to enter market:

While other cell manufacturers have been focusing on quality issues arising from conventional wet electrode technology, EIT has focused on dry electrode technology and partnered with the pioneers of this process.

Dry electrode is the most suitable technology for high performance batteries together with many other benefits such as energy, equipment and CO2 footprint reduction.

EIT is also designing its batteries based on tabless technology for immersion cooled packs.

Tabless design and immersion cooling are vital methods to deliver the highest performance in a safe manner by actively managing thermal outputs and enable higher charge/discharge rates without compromising battery life and safety.

While other manufacturers still require overcoming these technological barriers, EIT has first mover advantage.

Risk of other battery technologies:

Pure solid-state technology that is capable of employing lithium metal as anode is at least a decade away for commercialization with cycle life and fast charge issues to solve.

More advanced chemistries such as Li-S and Li-air are even in earlier infancy.

In the meantime, a lot of publicity has been centered around the so-called semi-solid technology.

These are mostly rebranding of the current technology that use electrolyte additives to suppress flammable characteristics of liquid electrolyte to be able to draw maximum performance available from existing electrode technology without thermal events.

Although these semi-solid cells may offer some degree of cell level performance optimization, they lack pack level performance criteria that is essential for their commercialization.

Practical barriers for other competitors to enter market

Market for high performance batteries is considerably smaller than mainstream battery market and many large manufacturers may not be attracted to it.

This is given the fact that, large manufacturers existing technology and production lines are not suitable to produce such batteries.

Many Asian manufacturers may be reluctant to invest in R&D to develop these cells as they may see it as demanding, small and geographically concentrated market.

Building a separate, smaller size factory close to those geographical destinations that is using a different technology and requires a modified production line with significant investment is not commercially viable from an opportunity cost perspective, given the size of the market.
